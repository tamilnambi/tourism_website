Thanjavur

Located in the heart of Tamil Nadu, the Thanjavur region is renowned for its rich cultural heritage and stunning architecture. Home to the Chola dynasty, the region is dotted with magnificent temples, beautiful palaces, and ancient monuments that bear witness to the region's glorious past. The region is also famous for its traditional arts and crafts, such as bronze casting, silk weaving, and stone carving. With its beautiful landscapes, rich history, and vibrant culture, the Thanjavur region is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Tamil Nadu.

Popular tourist places

Brihadeeswara Temple

This stunning temple, also known as the Big Temple, is one of the most iconic landmarks in Tamil Nadu. Built in the 11th century by the Chola dynasty, the temple is renowned for its magnificent architecture and intricate carvings.

Thanjavur Royal Palace

This magnificent palace was built by the Nayaks in the 16th century and is an excellent example of the traditional Dravidian style of architecture. The palace features a large courtyard, grand halls, and beautiful frescoes.

Saraswathi Mahal Library

This ancient library was founded by the Nayaks in the 16th century and is home to a vast collection of rare and ancient manuscripts, including palm-leaf manuscripts and books written on copper plates.

Gangaikonda Cholapuram

This ancient temple town was built in the 11th century by the Chola dynasty and is renowned for its magnificent temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. The town is also home to several other ancient temples and monuments.

Kumbakonam

This historic town is located on the banks of the River Kaveri and is famous for its beautiful temples and ancient monuments. The town is also known for its traditional arts and crafts, such as silk weaving and bronze casting.

Darasuram

This ancient temple town is located near Kumbakonam and is renowned for its magnificent Airavateswara Temple, which is dedicated to Lord Shiva. The temple is famous for its intricate carvings and stunning architecture, and is a must-visit for anyone traveling to the Thanjavur region.
